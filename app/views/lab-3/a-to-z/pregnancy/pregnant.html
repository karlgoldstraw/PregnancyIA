Being pregnant

This page provides links to all the pages and topics relating to your pregnacy care.

All pages in being pregnant

Early days

Find out everything you will need to know and do once you've confirmed that you're pregnant

Antenatal care

Antenatal care is the care you get from health professionals during your pregnancy. It's sometimes called pregnancy care or maternity care

Tests and scans

During your pregnancy, you'll be offered a range of tests, including blood tests and ultrasound baby scans

Pregnancy-induced conditions

Information about gestational diabetes, pre-eclampsia and Hyperemesis gravidarum (severe vomiting)

Food and drink in pregnancy

There are some foods to avoid or take care with when you're pregnant as they might make you ill or harm your baby

Existing health conditions

If you have an existing health condition there may be information that you need to consider. Find out what you need to know

Free prescriptions and more

Pregnant women are entitled to free prescriptions, dental treatment and other benefits

Illness during pregnancy

From food poisioning to chicken pox, get advice and how this may affect you and your baby

Lifestyle and wellbeing

There are things you can do, and things you can avoid, to keep you and your baby as healthy as possible in pregnancy

Medicines during pregnancy

Before taking any medicine when you're pregnant, including painkillers, check with your pharmacist, midwife or GP that it's suitable

Multiple pregnancies

Women with multiple pregnancies should be offered an ultrasound scan at around 11 to 14 weeks. It's important to attend this appointment

Partners

If you're the partner of a pregnant woman, the closer the two of you are, the more you'll be able to share the experience of pregnancy and birth

When pregnancy goes wrong

Sadly, sometimes pregnancy can go wrong. Women may have to face a miscarriage, an ectopic pregnancy or the death of the baby
