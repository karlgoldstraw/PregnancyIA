Diabetes

Diabetes is a lifelong condition that causes a person's blood sugar level to become too high.

During pregnancy, some women have such high levels of blood glucose that their body is unable to produce enough insulin to absorb it all. This is known as gestational diabetes.

However, there are two main types of diabetes:

Type 1 diabetes

Type 1 diabetes occurs when the body's immune system attacks and destroys the cells that produce insulin.

Type 2 diabetes

Type 2 diabetes occurs when where the body does not produce enough insulin, or the body's cells do not react to insulin.

Find out more

Pre-diabetes

Many more people have blood sugar levels above the normal range, but not high enough to be diagnosed as having diabetes.

Causes of diabetes

If you have diabetes, your body is unable to break down glucose into energy. This is because there's either not enough insulin to move the glucose, or the insulin produced does not work properly.

When to see a doctor

Visit your GP as soon as possible if you experience the main symptoms of diabetes. Type 1 diabetes can develop quickly, whereas Many people have type 2 diabetes for years without realising.
