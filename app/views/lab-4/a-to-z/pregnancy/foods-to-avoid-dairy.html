Food and drink to avoid in pregnancy

Dairy foods to avoid – pregnancy

Find out which dairy products you can and cannot eat or drink during pregnancy.

Some types of soft cheese are not safe to eat. This because they are more moist than other cheeses and allow bacteria to grow, causing listeria.

Cheese you should not eat

Soft, mould-ripened cheeses are not safe to eat, whether they’re made from pasteurised milk or not.

Do not eat:

  • do not eat brie
  • do not eat camembert
  • do not eat chevre
  • do not eat taleggio
  • do not eat cambozola

Soft, blue cheese are not safe to eat whether they’re made from pasteurised cheese or not.

Do not eat:

  • do not eat danish blue
  • do not eat gorgonzola
  • do not eat roquefort
  • do not eat docelatte
  • do not eat blue wendsleydale
  • do not eat blue tomme

Cheese you can eat

All hard cheeses are safe to eat when you’re pregnant, even if they’re made from non-pasteurised milk.

You can eat:

  • cheddar
  • carphilly
  • edam
  • gauda
  • gruyere
  • provolone
  • parmesan and pecorino
  • manchego

Soft, processed cheeses made with pasteurised milk are safe to eat (check the label).

You can eat:

  • feta
  • goats cheese with no rind
  • halloumi
  • mozzerella
  • paneer
  • ricotta
  • parmesan and pecorino
  • cottage cheese
  • cream cheese

Risks of eating some dairy

Some types of soft cheese are not safe to eat because they are more moist than other cheeses and allow bacteria to grow, causing listeria.

This is rare, but it can lead to miscarriage, still birth or serious illness in a newborn baby.

If you’re pregnant and are worried you may have the symptoms of listeria, speak to a GP or midwife straight away.
